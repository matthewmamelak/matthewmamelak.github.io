About Me

Hi everyone! My name is Matthew Mamelak and I am in my third year of Computer Engineering at Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario. Ever since a young age, I have always been very interested in technology and computers. In high school, I was enrolled in a four-year engineering specialization stream. During that time I came to the realization that I love learning about computer technology and I am now very interested in pursuing a career in an AI, e-commerce or fintech company.

During my undergraduate studies, I have taken several courses in Python as well as Java and C/C++ and I am very comfortable programming in these computer languages. Most of my course work has centred around problem-solving and I believe that I have learned to analyze data effectively, break problems down into components and come up with creative solutions. My course work has required critical thinking and has taught me how to organize my work and prioritize multiple projects at a time. I have learned how to present data effectively to my teachers and colleagues and how to communicate my ideas to others. As my course work has been team-based, I have learned about team dynamics, and have come to recognize that I really enjoy working with others.

The rigorous nature of the computer engineering program at Queen’s has forced me to develop effective time management skills which were further strengthened by my recent work experiences. The skills I have gained over the past two years have helped me succeed as a summer intern working as a Business Automation Analyst at TD bank. I enjoy applying the computer programming skills that I have learned in my coursework to the discovery of novel solutions to real world problems.

Aside from the “tech” component of my life, I love to play sports and have played competitive hockey and tennis my entire life. I play on an intramural hockey team at school, and I play pickup tennis whenever I can. I also play other intramural sports like baseball and volleyball.

Projects

OepnSeat - QHacks 2023

- A real-time library occupancy tracker using affordable NFC tags.
- Easily locates available seats, saving students valuable study time during busy hours.
- Students tap NFC tags with smartphones to mark seats as OCCUPIED or AVAILABLE.
- Developed front-end using HTML and CSS, and back-end functionality using PHP, SQL, and an Apache server connected to the Internet via ngrok.

Activity Classifier

- Collaborated with a team of students to develop an activity classifier that detects if the person is walking or jumping.
- To accomplish this, we created a logisitic regression classifier and trained it with our own raw data.
- We then performed webscraping using Selenium and Beautiful Soup to extract the live results from a specific URL.
- Finally, we built a simple GUI in python using PyQt to display the output

Click here to open a PDF version of the report.

Rocket Engine Test Stand Design

- Collaborated with a team of students to develop a rocket engine test stand for the Queen's Rocket Engineering Team.
- Coded a force sensor using Arduino and demonstrated adaptability by modelling the sensor's performance using SolidWorks.
- Assumed a leadership role as team co-lead by assigning tasks to other members, organizing meetings with the client, and proofreading documents for accuracy and completeness.

Click here to open a PDF version of the report.

Emergency Fall Detection Device

- Worked with a group of five students to program an emergency fall detection device used to alert an emergency contact if it detects a sudden change in acceleration.
- Successfully incorporated sensors, a data processing unit, and a telecommunication device.
- Oversaw the programming of the accelerometer, Bluetooth module, buttons, LCD screen, and buzzer using Arduino software.

C++ Casino Game

- Worked with a group of students to develop a functioning, casino Game (using wxWidgets for our GUI).
- The game can run on Mac or Windows computers. The GUI includes different routes for each of the three games.
- The three games we have created are Blackjack, Roulette, and Odds Are. All variables were saved saved locally to avoid hosting a database.
